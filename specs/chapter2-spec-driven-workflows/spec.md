# Chapter 2: Spec-Driven Development Workflows - Specification

## Overview
This document specifies the content, structure, and requirements for Chapter 2 of the Teaching AI Native Book, titled "Spec-Driven Development Workflows". This chapter focuses on the methodology of creating detailed specifications before implementation in AI-native development, emphasizing how structured specifications enable effective human-AI collaboration.

## Chapter Purpose
To provide readers with a comprehensive understanding of spec-driven development workflows in AI-native software development. The chapter will demonstrate how detailed specifications serve as the foundation for effective collaboration between humans and AI systems, enabling reproducible and high-quality development processes.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this chapter, readers will be able to:
- Define spec-driven development and its role in AI-native workflows
- Create detailed, unambiguous specifications that both humans and AI can understand
- Apply structured approaches to specification writing for different types of development tasks
- Evaluate the quality of specifications for AI-native development
- Implement iterative refinement processes for specifications
- Recognize the relationship between good specifications and successful AI collaboration

## Target Audience
- University-level computer science and software engineering students
- Educators teaching modern software development practices
- Developers transitioning to AI-native workflows

## Scope
### Inclusions
- Definition and principles of spec-driven development
- The role of specifications in human-AI collaboration
- Techniques for writing AI-comprehensible specifications
- Specification templates and patterns
- Examples of good vs. poor specifications
- Iterative specification refinement processes
- Tools and practices for specification management
- Case studies demonstrating spec-driven workflows
- Quality metrics for specifications
- Integration of specifications with AI development tools

### Exclusions
- Detailed programming language syntax (examples should be accessible to multi-language developers)
- Vendor-specific tool tutorials (focus on general principles)
- Deep technical implementation details of AI models
- Project management methodologies beyond specification creation
- Advanced mathematical concepts underlying AI systems
